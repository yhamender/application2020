Task 2: Measure the four critical areas of your school campus. Task 2: Identify the types of areas in your school.
To find out the land/ site area of your school, you need to measure:
  • Ground coverage area
  • Open Area (on-ground)
  • Service Area (on-ground)
  • Service Parking

1. Site area in square meter: Site area refers to the area or exact plot of ground on which anything is, has been, or is to be located. ‘Plot’ means a piece of land enclosed by definite boundaries.
2.Ground Coverage Area: The audit team will have to get in touch with the administrative staff in the school office to procure the electricity bills.
3. Green cover or green, landscaped area on-ground: this includes areas covered by grass, trees, lawn or horticulture, flower beds, grasscrete paver tiles, etc.
4. Surface parking area: refers to any parking lots directly on land.
5. Play area that is paved/ concrete: this includes all outdoor playfields such as basketball court; skating rink; assembly ground; swimming pool, etc.
6. Play area covered by grass on ground: this includes tennis grass court, cricket/ football grounds etc.
7. Service area on ground: this is any area on ground that is not a permanent structure, but dedicated to services such as cooling towers for air conditioning, generators, portable cabins for security guards, etc.
8. Roof and terrace area: A roof is the covering on the uppermost part of a building. A terrace is an outdoor, occupiable extension of a building above ground level.
9. Green cover on terrace/ balconies: this includes terrace garden, but not potted plants.
10. Total built up/ constructed area: this refers to the total areas of all floors of the building, including ground floors of built-up spaces, which include classrooms, labs, staff rooms, office area, auditoria, corridors, as well as balconies and terraces, multiplied by the number of floors.
Others: this will include paved paths, and other open area that your school has, but not covered in the above list.
To collect data: The total constructed area can be sourced from the building construction plan of the school, which will be available in the school office. Or else, measure the constructed spaces and use the formula for calculating the areas of various shapes, such as a square or rectangle, to derive the data and add them all.

Task 3: Explore the number of species of plants and animals in your school
This task will ... help you to understand the number and variety of plants and animals that your school campus supports. Identify and count the various species of plants and animals in your school.
To collect data:
Make two teams of students. One for plant species and the other for animal species. Get the school gardener and the biology teacher to accompany them. Count plant species and animal species that are visible to the naked eye.
Native (definition): a species that occurs naturally within a region, either evolving there or arriving and becoming established without human assistance
Exotic (definition): An exotic plant or animal, which is invasive, is a species that meets the following conditions:
1. Originate outside the local area.
2. Have the potential to spread long distances on its own.
3. Once established, have the potential to out-compete native species in the wild.
